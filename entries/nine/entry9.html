Entry 9: “My uncle passed away when I was two weeks old.”

DATE

02/11/25, around 9pm

SETTING

In one of the Parsons' sewing rooms, two girls are talking about going to Church and their faith, and the Christian community in the New School.

PEOPLE

The girl whos is speaking has dirty blonde hair (or brown hair bleached blonde), wearing white headphones around her neck and a matching black hoodie and sweatpants set that had rhinestones on them.
The other girl had long black hair with downturned eyes and sharp eyebrows.

My uncle passed away when I was two weeks old.

It was three days before Christmas, and he had been drunk driving.

He was getting involved in some gangs because some of his friends were working for them, so he was naturally roped in.

My dad was really close to him actually, he was like this older brother figure to him.

He had sat my uncle down and convinced him to stop hanging out with these people, and getting involved with the gangs.

There was a girl he was seeing for a long time, like almost seven years, I think.

And a week before he passed, he actually proposed to her, but she turned him down and broke up with him.

He started drinking a lot and going to a bunch of parties and everything.

My dad and my mom were like, “You should come to us to this Christmas party,” and they were still young parents, so it was still a fun, cute Christmas.

And they had fought him basically for his keys because he had driven home with some girl he met at a party.

He and my dad actually physically fought before he got in his car and drove away.

And… and my dad had to identify him.

Somebody had seen a horrible car crash and told them about it.

He had to tell my mom ‘cause he just knew it was him, even though it hadn’t been confirmed yet.

She had to be put on antidepressants when I was five years old, but even now, it’s so fucked up

They were all there, they were all there, so freaky…

When he passed, my grandma participated more, but my grandpa, his death completely destroyed his faith in God…

SCENE END.
